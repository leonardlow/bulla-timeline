# Bulla Timeline

An interactive genealogical timeline weaving together the Low family lineage with Hawaiian, American, and European history spanning 255 years (1756–2011).

## Overview

This project explores the Low family's place in history—from **Kapokini** (born 1756 in Pololu Valley, Kohala) to **Levi Laakea Leen Kamahao Holmbeck Low** (born 2011). The timeline interweaves family milestones with pivotal historical moments:

- **Hawaiian History**: Kingdom era, Unification (1795), Overthrow (1893), Annexation (1898), Statehood (1959)
- **American History**: Revolution, Civil War, Gold Rush, Pearl Harbor, WWII
- **European History**: Enlightenment-era events and transformations
- **Family Events**: Births, marriages, and the arc of the Low lineage across generations

## Key Insights

- Your family's roots extend from pre-unification Hawaii through American statehood
- Julia Keliimanaole (Great×2 Grandmother) witnessed the Overthrow at age 43
- Emma Kaleiaukai (Grandmother) lived through Pearl Harbor, WWII, and Hawaii's transition to statehood
- The family moved from Kohala to Honolulu during the territorial era, adapting to changing times
- Levi's birth (2011) represents the 9th generation in documented lineage

## How to Use

Open `index.html` in any modern web browser. Click on any event to expand its description. Events are color-coded:

- 🔴 **Red**: Family Events
- 🔵 **Teal**: Hawaiian History
- 🟡 **Yellow**: American History
- 🟢 **Green**: European History
- 🔶 **Pink**: Sports History
